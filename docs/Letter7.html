Letter 7

In Denmark

  • July 26th-27th, 1955
  • John M. Lilley Library, Penn State Behrend Archives
  • Editors:
    • AMA
    • MRS
  • Summary: This letter begins with a handwritten portion that was illegible to us, but we hope to work on transcribing it in the future. "Denmark" is legibly written in the margin. The rest of the letter describes a lively day of entertainment, shopping for unique crafts, and enjoying a fine dinner, capped off with a circus show featuring elephants and acrobats.
Denmark July 26th














Very gay place. Music and all sorts of
places to spend cash for fun. Gail shot bears and bulls
eyes and smashed up plates. It is truly a nice place.
Everyone very orderly and enjoying it.


July 27th.
Went to the Travel Agency. Went over to the d'angleterre (No Rooms)
Hotel, and to an art craft place next to it where
I got some wonderful things! Lions made of rope, lunch
doilies with designs of all nations. Things for Kissie,
a beaded bracelet, Gloves, etc. Had lunch the d'Angle
terre. Couldn't get rooms there- all full. Went to the
Permente- a place thet shows all sorts of things to buy
Got a skirt, linen table cloth, scarves, fish doilies,
At 4.30 we went on the city tour In a bus. all around.
Back to hotel- flowers from Mr and Mrs Dessau. They
had called. Went to the Coq D'Or and had a very fine
dinner. On way back to hotel passed the Circus Schuman
WE couldn't get in- laws that prevent when it is full
up- but a very nice attendant let us stand upstairs
behind the rows of people. So we saw a lot of it and
it was a grand show. Elephants, acrobats and all
sorts of acts.
